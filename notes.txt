‚úÖ 1. Button Hierarchy (Primary vs Secondary vs Tertiary)
Popular e-commerce sites usually follow button hierarchy rules:

Type	Style Example	Use Case
Primary	Solid (e.g., black or brand color)	Main actions (e.g., ‚ÄúAdd to Cart‚Äù, ‚ÄúBuy Now‚Äù)
Secondary	Outline or ghost with border	Less important actions (e.g., ‚ÄúExplore‚Äù, ‚ÄúRead More‚Äù)
Tertiary	Text-only or very light ghost	Least important (e.g., ‚ÄúLearn More‚Äù, links)

In your screenshot, all buttons are solid black, which may confuse the user about what action is most important.
